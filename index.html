During Covid

Nolenopo I

Sidenote: Sorry for the poor audio & quality in general

Content

The game itself was fairly simple: There were five levels that I designed with MagicaVoxel. Each level had zombies trying to kill you. You had to escape them for a certain amount of time to win. You could buy items with coins you got from completing levels. I also added a multiplayer mode, but never finished it (all you could do was walk around and chat, although that alone wasn't easy)

Context: Personal Project

This was the first time I ever *really* tried to make a video game - It was basically just a project for fun and learning, but it's sentimental to me since it was my first ever standalone project and the first project I ever showed to others. I made it during COVID, so there was no school to worry about

Note: In the video I claimed that was the first time I used Unity, which was incorrect - I had used Unity in the past, I had just never actually made anything in it

Nolenopo V3

Content

The game takes place in a 2d world which extends 200 blocks in either direction. The goal is to survive as long as possible against the zombies, who spawn near you. They spawn at a higher rate in the purple biome.

Context: School Project

This was my Computer Science final for freshman year, in 2022. We chose our own project, I initially intended to make a strategy game but decided to make this instead after conceptualizing the world generation in math class. This took about a month to make and is 1300 lines long, in Javascript. I made all the textures and stuff myself.

Legacy of Solaris V1

Content

This game consists of cities and characters on a map. You get tax income from the cities, which you can use to buy soldiers. You can declare wars with other cities and attack their soldiers.

Context: Camp Project

This was my final camp project. I had nine days to work on it and I came up with the idea for it myself, but it was *for* camp. After coming back for camp, I stopped developing this version but I began an entirely new version seperately.
